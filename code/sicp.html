For the past year or so, I have been slowly working my way through Sussman’s, Sussman’s, and Ableson’s iconic text. This book was my introduction to the idea of functional programming and the greater LISP family of languages. Being a sucker for simple concepts that can be combined into great complexities, I fell in love with the bare syntax and core concept of LISP’s s-expressions. In spite of this beauty, however, I eventually gave up on using LISP in most of my projects for the standard litany of reasons in favor of learning Haskell.
